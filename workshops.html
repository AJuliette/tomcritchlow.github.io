The Tensions of Modern Marketing

Every business I work with seems to currently struggle along these tensions:

Mediabrands

In the distributed and social world we live in there’s a tension between brands, who specialize in user acquisition, user insights and monetization. And media companies, who specialize in editorial, narrative, story and (often) purpose. Brands lack distribution and interestingness Media companies lack user insights, or a notion of branding. (And there’s a middle grey area of affiliate businesses that generate content at the scale of media properties but are more savvy on the commercials while being less editorially interesting).

Brand v Acquisition

When was the last time the VP of Brand spoke to the VP of growth? Brand marketing (and brand marketers) excel at positioning a brand into something relevant. But they lack the data fluency and action-oriented mindset of a growth team. Activities like paid acquisition, SEO and content marketing often fall under the leadership of data driven marketers, while the brand is somewhere else trying to move a different set of KPIs. But in today’s world we reach users in a diverse set of always-on channels and need brand and performance to work in unison.

Systems of Insight

We must balance carefully the tension between the ill-defined and abstract work of gathering insights about our users and audience with the formulaic and scalable process of executing marketing activities. In today’s technology landscape we need to build system of insight to gather feedback loops between users and our product and marketing.

Purpose v Process

Marketing is ruled by processes. But to better push decision making to the edges - top down marketing strategy is failed strategy.

Measurable Creativity

The greatest trick the devil ever played was helping marketing become “measurable”. We can measure ever smaller pulsating cells of atomic marketing but struggle to reconcile with ambitious and different marketing campaigns. The most effective thing we can do is the thing that’s never been seen before. Conversely how can we bring more rigour to the abstract creative nature of content, brand and editorial?

What I've Learned Running Workshops

Workshops are overengineered for comfort.
Too many workshops have rigid formats and don’t allow for free ranging conversations. The way I work is often more like sparring and back and forth.
You can’t “solve” much in a one or two day workshop.
The aim of a workshop is to get to new ways of seeing. Solutions and breakthroughs often drop out but they’re not the goal.
Experiments are deliverables
Immediate value is often realized from creating a shared set of experiments to validate - MVPs of campaigns, project structure, hiring and more
